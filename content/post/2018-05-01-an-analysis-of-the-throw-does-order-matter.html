Differences in treble accuracy

Figure 1: 95% confidence intervals for the binomial proportion for each player to successfully hit a treble with their first, second and third darts.

Michael Smith appears to have a statistically significant difference in the probability of successfully hitting a treble with his first and second darts (the confidence intervals donâ€™t overlap in the figure above indicating a statistical significance). The probability is higher for him to successfully hit a treble with his second dart, than it is for him to successfully hit a treble with his first dart.

Interestingly, Michael Smith is the only player who shows any sign of a (statistically) significant difference in the probability of successfully hitting a treble with his 3 darts.

Differences in double accuracy

Figure 2: 95% confidence intervals for the binomial proportion for each player to successfully hit a double with their first, second and third darts.

There are no statistically significant differences in the probability of any player to successfully hit the double with their first, second, or third darts. Despite this, it appears that Gary Anderson may have a slightly lower probability of successfully hitting a double with his last dart, whereas Michael van Gerwen appears to have a slightly lower probability of successfully hitting the double with his first dart.

Differences in bullseye accuracy

Figure 3: 95% confidence intervals for the binomial proportion for each player to successfully hit a bullseye with their first, second and third darts.

There are no statistically significant differences in the probability of any player to successfully hit the bullseye with their first, second, or third darts.
